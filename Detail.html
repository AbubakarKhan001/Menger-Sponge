What is the Menger Sponge?

The Menger Sponge is a 3D fractal that is built by recursively subdividing a cube. It is an example of a geometric object that has a non-integer dimension, a characteristic of fractals. The Menger Sponge is often used to demonstrate concepts in mathematical fractals, self-similarity, and dimension theory.

The construction process starts with a solid cube. Then, at each step, you remove smaller cubes from the structure, leaving behind a hollow, sponge-like shape. The more iterations you do, the more complex the structure becomes.

This fractal is a part of the family of Menger Curves, and its dimension lies between 2 and 3, making it a fascinating example of objects that do not fit neatly into the traditional definitions of dimensions.
